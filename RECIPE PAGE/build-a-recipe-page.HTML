Coconut Cardamom Mandazi
(East African Beignets)

Mandazi is a popular East African snack, especially in Uganda, Kenya, and Tanzania. These soft and slightly sweet fried dough triangles are similar to doughnuts but lighter, often made with coconut milk and warm spices like cardamom.
They’re perfect for breakfast, tea-time, or as a quick treat. Mandazi can be enjoyed plain or paired with tea, coffee, or even sprinkled with powdered sugar.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • ½ cup sugar
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • ½ tsp ground cardamom (optional but recommended)
  • ¼ tsp salt
  • ¾ cup coconut milk (or regular milk)
  • 1 egg (optional, for softer texture)
  • 2 tbsp margarine or butter (softened)
  • Oil for deep frying

Instructions

  1. In a mixing bowl, combine flour, sugar, baking powder, cardamom, and salt.
  2. Add margarine/butter and rub into the dry mixture using your fingertips.
  3. Whisk the egg and coconut milk together, then gradually add to the flour mixture.
  4. Mix until a soft dough forms. If too sticky, add a little flour.
  5. Cover the dough and let it rest for 10–15 minutes.
  6. Roll out the dough to about ½-inch thickness.
  7. Cut into triangles or your preferred shape.
  8. Heat oil in a deep pan on medium heat.
  9. Fry the mandazi until golden brown on both sides, turning occasionally.
  10. Remove and drain on paper towels.
  11. Serve warm. Enjoy!
